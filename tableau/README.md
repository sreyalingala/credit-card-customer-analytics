# Credit Card Customer Churn Dashboard

This project presents an interactive Tableau dashboard built to analyze customer churn behavior in a credit card portfolio. The goal of the dashboard is to identify patterns in customer attrition and highlight factors that may contribute to customers leaving the bank.

The dataset contains information about customer demographics, account activity, credit card usage, and tenure with the bank. By aggregating and visualizing this data, the dashboard helps surface trends that can support retention strategies and business decision making.

## Dashboard Overview

The dashboard summarizes key churn indicators and customer behavior patterns through a set of focused visualizations. At the top of the dashboard, KPI metrics provide a quick overview of the customer base, including the total number of customers, average transaction amount, and overall churn rate.

The visualizations then explore churn across several important dimensions:

- **Attrition Distribution** shows the proportion of existing vs. attrited customers.
- **Churn Rate by Card Category** compares churn across different card types.
- **Churn Rate by Number of Products** highlights how product engagement relates to churn.
- **Average Transaction Amount** compares spending behavior between attrited and existing customers.
- **Customer Tenure Analysis** explores how churn varies across different stages of the customer lifecycle.

Together, these views help reveal behavioral patterns such as lower spending activity and lower product engagement among customers who churn.

## Key Insights

Some patterns observed from the analysis include:

- The overall churn rate is approximately **16%**, indicating a significant portion of customers leaving the portfolio.
- Customers holding **fewer products** tend to show higher churn rates compared to those with multiple products.
- **Lower transaction activity** is often associated with attrited customers.
- Churn patterns vary across **different card categories**, suggesting differences in customer engagement levels.

These insights can help guide strategies focused on customer retention, product bundling, and engagement improvement.

## Tools Used
